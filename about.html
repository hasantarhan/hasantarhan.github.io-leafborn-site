The maker · solo · indie · Türkiye

One hand,
a thousand leaves.

Leafborn is made by one person, carefully, across years. No publisher. No team. No roadmap beyond making it the best it can be.

Developer Hasan Tarhan

Leafborn began as a question: what would a character feel like if it didn't have a body? What if every motion were a physical event — a thousand leaves reacting, scattering, gathering?

That question became a prototype. The prototype became a year. The year became this: a 3D roguelite action, designed and built by one person in Türkiye, on Unity. I write the code. I tune the physics. I cut the trailers.

I'm not building a game I hope you'll play. I'm building a game I can't stop thinking about, and hoping that shows.

I work best slowly. Leafborn's rate of progress is measured in felt quality, not milestones. The wishlist matters. The Discord matters more — a small room full of people who care is worth more than a thousand silent metrics.
